About the Data
This interactive map displays drought conditions across the United States for the first week of each month from 2020 to present. The data comes from the U.S. Drought Monitor, which classifies drought based on factors such as precipitation, temperature, soil moisture, and streamflow. Categories range from D0 (Abnormally Dry) to D4 (Exceptional Drought), capturing both short and long term drought impacts across diverse regions and time periods.

Data source: U.S. Drought Monitor — National Drought Mitigation Center (NDMC), U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA), and National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA).
